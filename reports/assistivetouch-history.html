Documenting Apple's AssistiveTouch

Motivation

AssistiveTouch is one of Apple's most significant inclusive design features and it serves millions of users with motor impairments. Despite its critical role, documentation about its history, features, impact on mobile interaction techniques, and reception by disabled users is fragmented in the public domain.

My goal was to close this knowledge gap by creating a comprehensive and accessible Wikipedia entry. To do this, I needed to consolidate scattered documentations and user feedback into one reliable source to inform users and designers about such a pervasive accessibility tool.

Research Process

Project Justification

Before drafting the article, I audited reports and documentation about AssistiveTech to justify the value of the project. I specifically looked for real-world impact and design history. I found that AssistiveTouch was one of the first widespread accessibility initiatives embedded directly into a mobile OS. It set a standard for inclusive design over the last decade, and documenting it provides historical context for future design decisions.

Iteration

The authoring process was a deep dive into technical writing for a public audience. I needed to be both rigorous in my explanations but also concise and use accessible language. I worked through multiple drafts, iterating through versions that used technical language into easily digestible information without extraneous jargon. This required finding a balanced spot between excessively documenting a complex interaction technique and simply reporting the features of an accessibility tool.

This process demanded discipline in following Wikipedia's standards. I had to master the Wiki text editor syntax and apply citation templates to ensure every piece of information was properly validated while following guidelines on how to neutrally and concisely cover information.

I collected all necessary visuals as original media (screenshots and an animated GIF demonstrating the feature in use) to fully comply with licensing requirements. I wrote detailed alternative text for every image, guaranteeing that screen reader users would have full access to the article's visual content.

Reflection

This project deepened my understanding of how to document an interaction technique through research, technical writing, and iterative review. My goal was to create a comprehensive knowledge base accessible to everyone. It taught me the importance of distinguishing essential facts from extraneous details to maintain clarity, and filtering through reviews of the product to determine which were appropriate and credible to include.


The draft had been initially submitted for review in May 2025, but was rejected for extraneous sections and insufficient citations. This provided a learning opportunity in scoping and iteration. I revised the article, and have submitted again, and the draft was approved and published in November 2025! Currently, I am working on
